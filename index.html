Think Before You Click: Cybersecurity Awareness for Students

Introduction

Cybersecurity is crucial for students who spend a lot of time online. Being aware of the risks associated with online activities can help protect your personal information and avoid falling victim to cybercrimes.

Phishing

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers try to trick you into giving away personal information, like passwords or credit card numbers. Always verify the source before clicking on a link or opening an email attachment.

How to Spot a Phishing Attempt:

  • Look for suspicious email addresses.
  • Check for spelling and grammar mistakes.
  • Never click on suspicious links or attachments.

Safe Browsing Habits

Always ensure you are browsing secure websites by checking for "https" and a padlock icon in the browser's address bar. Avoid clicking on pop-up ads or downloading unknown files.

Safe Browsing Checklist:

  1. Use strong passwords and update them regularly.
  2. Enable two-factor authentication when available.
  3. Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi networks.

Social Media Safety

On social media, avoid sharing too much personal information. Cybercriminals can use it to trick you into revealing sensitive data. Review your privacy settings and limit what you share publicly.

Other Online Scams

Beware of fraudulent schemes like fake job offers or investment scams. Always verify the legitimacy of offers before providing any personal information.
